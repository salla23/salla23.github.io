Basketball Game Understanding (in progress)

Tracking & re-ID · team assignment · court homography · github ↗

A computer-vision system for turning basketball footage into structured game information — who is on the court, which team they belong to, where they are, and what number they wear. It is the passion project I keep coming back to, and it happens to be a clean container for the perception problems I care about most: identity under occlusion and recovering geometry from a single camera.

Problem

Basketball is adversarial for trackers. Players wear near-identical uniforms, cluster tightly, and constantly cross and screen each other, so identities swap exactly when two players are closest. And a broadcast frame is a perspective projection of a flat court, so on-screen pixels say little about real position until that projection is undone.

Approach

Detect players, the ball, and jersey numbers; track them with re-identification that survives occlusion by pairing motion prediction with an appearance model, so a player who disappears behind a screen keeps the same identity when they re-emerge. Separate the two teams by clustering uniform appearance without manual labels. And estimate the court-to-image homography from court lines, so every player's pixel position maps to a real coordinate on the floor — the step that turns tracking into analysis: spacing, movement, and possession geometry.

Status

The pipeline structure and the detection and clustering front end are in place; the occlusion-robust re-identification and the robust court homography are in progress, alongside the analysis layer built on top of them.
