Reading

Papers and books that shaped how I think — and why each stuck

A running list, grouped by theme. Texts that have changed how I approach a problem.

3D Reconstruction & Image Stitching

BIMStruct3D: A Fully Automated Hybrid Learning Scan-to-BIM Pipeline with Integrated Topology Refinement paper
Chamseddine, Kaufmann, Schellen, Glock, Stricker, Rambach · EC³ (European Conference on Computing in Construction) · 2026
The work my scan-to-BIM pipeline builds on; its instance-matching-free voxel-overlap metric (vIoU) is what I adopted so I could compare reconstructed geometry to ground truth without brittle per-instance 3D-IoU.
Monocular Depth Estimation Based on Deep Learning: An Overview paper
Zhao, Sun, Zhang, Tang, Qian · arXiv · 2020
The map I return to for where learned monocular depth helps and where multi-view geometry still wins — directly relevant to the VSLAM-plus-triangulation depth work I built.
Automated Building Image Extraction from 360° Panoramas for Post-Disaster Evaluation paper
Lenjani et al. · arXiv · 2019
Panorama retrieval plus CV for structural assessment; it connects to my interest in stitching wide views and recovering a usable perspective from inside them.

Perception in Messy Conditions

RDNet — Raindrop Detection Network whitepaper
EDAG Engineering · Whitepaper · 2021 · building on Hao et al. (2019), "Learning From Synthetic Photorealistic Raindrop for Single Image Raindrop Removal"
Adverse-weather camera perception done sensibly: segment the raindrops, then reconstruct what they hide. The synthetic-to-real generalization result is the part that stuck.
Water Segmentation with Deep Learning for Flood Detection and Monitoring paper
Zaffaroni & Rossi · LINKS Foundation · year to confirm
Segmentation under genuinely messy field conditions, where deployment constraints matter as much as the architecture — close in spirit to the road-surface work I care about.

Evaluation & Honest Metrics

Combating Label Noise in Deep Learning Using Abstention paper
Thulasidasan et al. · NeurIPS · 2019
A loss that lets a network decline to predict on confusing samples is the cleanest version of an idea I keep returning to: knowing when a model should abstain is part of honest evaluation, not an afterthought.
Voxel-based overlap (vIoU) for Scan-to-BIM evaluation method
introduced in BIMStruct3D (Chamseddine et al., 2026)
Instance-matching-free overlap is the metric that finally let me evaluate reconstructed walls honestly — per-instance 3D-IoU collapses on a wall-thickness offset and hides exactly the errors that matter.
Boosted Decision Trees paper
Coadou · in "AI for High Energy Physics" · 2022
The clearest treatment of classifier figures-of-merit (ROC, significance) I've found; I keep it as a refresher on evaluation discipline as much as on the algorithm.

Forecasting, Causal & Production ML

Concurrent Neural Network: A Model of Competition Between Time Series paper
Garnier · arXiv · 2020
A principled way to model cannibalization between competing products — exactly the confounder I had to control for in price-elasticity and forecasting across retail markets.
Time Series Clustering for Grouping Products by Price and Sales Patterns paper
Bozanta et al. · arXiv · 2022
The custom distance metric (how price and sales move together, not raw values) reframed how I'd segment products before forecasting them.
A State-Space Approach to Estimate the Energy Demand Model paper
Tong & Yang · Energy Procedia · citation to confirm
A compact reminder of why state-space / UCM formulations fit demand series with structural components so well — the backbone of the forecasting I shipped.
A Gradient Boosting Approach for Optimal Selection of Bidding Strategies in Reservoir Hydro paper
Riddervold et al. · arXiv · 2020
Using ML to choose a strategy under uncertainty, rather than just predict a number, is the decision-layer framing I find more honest than chasing point-forecast accuracy.

Foundations & Methods

Rapid Object Detection Using a Boosted Cascade of Simple Features paper
Viola & Jones · CVPR · 2001
The classic that taught me cascades and cost-aware early rejection; still my reference point when a classical detector has to run in real time on weak hardware.
An Attention-Infused Deep Learning System with Grad-CAM for Early Glaucoma Screening paper
Swaminathan · arXiv · 2025
A concrete CNN-plus-Vision-Transformer cross-attention build with Grad-CAM interpretability; part of how I'm closing my own gap on transformer-based vision.

Game AI & Agents (outside work)

Winning Isn't Everything: Enhancing Game Development with Intelligent Agents paper
~2020
Agents tuned for playtesting and balance rather than raw win-rate — a refreshing reframe of what "good" means for an agent, and why I tinker with game AI on the side.
Genetic Algorithms for Evolving Computer Chess Programs paper
David, van den Herik, Koppel, Netanyahu
Evolving an evaluation function from grandmaster games up to grandmaster strength is a beautiful demonstration of learning structure from data — and pure fun to read.

Books

Robotics: Control, Sensing, Vision, and Intelligence textbook
Fu, Gonzalez & Lee · McGraw-Hill
The reference I still pull off the shelf for kinematics, sensing, and the vision-to-control bridge; a lot of how I frame robotics problems traces back to it.
Into Thin Air book
Jon Krakauer · 1997
A clear-eyed account of how small decisions compound into failure under pressure. If/when I get the oppurtunity to climb everest, I hope to be wiser for it.
The World Without Us book
Alan Weisman · 2007
A thought experiment about what persists when the operators (humans) leave.
