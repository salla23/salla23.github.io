About

Eight years of perception systems for the physical world

I build computer vision and perception systems that have to survive contact with the real world — messy sensor data, edge hardware, and operators who need the system to work, not to score well on a benchmark. The through-line across my work is an evaluation-first habit: define what "correct" actually means for the problem before reaching for a model, then hold the system to that definition honestly.

Where I've worked

ICURO — Founding Autonomous Systems Engineer. My strongest anchor in perception and robotics. I built a multi-sensor perception and localization stack for a teleoperated platform: a four-sensor suite fusing camera VSLAM, fiducial pose, sonar, and 2D LiDAR through an EKF with confidence-weighted failover, plus a probabilistic occupancy grid consumed by the planner. I shipped edge vision on Jetson with a roughly 70% latency reduction, and the systems I built there are still in daily production use by hundreds of operators.

Applied Materials — Machine Vision Engineer. My evaluation-discipline anchor. I solved an SEM defect-detection and clustering problem that three prior engineering teams had failed to crack — not with a bigger model, but by defining domain-grounded correctness metrics first, then building to them. I documented the approach in an internal IEEE-style paper.

Kimberly-Clark — AI Tech Lead. Several years of production ML discipline: price-elasticity and forecasting models with explicit causal-confounder control, promotion optimization, and drift monitoring with automated retraining. I led a small team of machine learning engineers and data scientists delivering models across six international markets.

Independent (2026–present). Two client engagements in classical 3D perception: a scan-to-BIM reconstruction pipeline converting 225M+ point laser scans of a building into a structured IFC model, and an embedded road-defect detection and navigation system. I also teach AI for Robotics part-time through UC Santa Cruz Silicon Valley Extension, covering perception, SLAM, and deployment.

How I work

I care about hard physical-world ML problems and the unglamorous engineering that makes a system survivable in production — the part most demos skip. I trust production usability and operator reliability more than headline model metrics, and I'm more interested in the audit that catches a confident-but-wrong result than in a clean-looking summary number. Geometry and classical methods first where they're enough; learning where geometry runs out.

Education & foundations

B.S. in Computer Engineering, California State University, Sacramento (2018), Tau Beta Pi. Primary languages Python and C++; deep work in 3D scene understanding, visual SLAM and structure-from-motion, multi-view geometry, semantic segmentation, and edge deployment (ONNX, TensorRT, CUDA, Jetson).
