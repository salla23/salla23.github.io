About

I build computer vision and perception systems that survive contact with the real world. At Applied Materials, I solved a defect-clustering problem on SEM imagery that three prior teams couldn't solve, by defining what "correct" meant before I touched a model. At ICURO, I shipped edge vision on Jetson Xavier with a 70% latency cut, and the 3D perception systems I built reached daily production use by hundreds of operators. For the last several years I led AI/ML development and delivery at Kimberly-Clark, running production models across six international markets. I care about hard physical-world ML problems and the unglamorous engineering that makes a system survivable in production. I trust production usability and operator reliability more than model metrics. More about me →

EKF Localization + Occupancy Mapping

github ↗
  • From-scratch 3-DoF EKF fusing visual odometry and fiducial pose with adaptive, quality-weighted measurement covariance and Joseph-form updates.
  • Graceful failover: a dropped fiducial is a skipped update — the filter coasts and recovers.
  • Log-odds occupancy mapping from ray-cast LiDAR/sonar, a semantic layer, and a ROS2 node layer. Validated in simulation with a full unit and integration test suite.

Crack Detection & Autonomous Navigation Pipeline

github ↗
  • Hybrid pipeline: CLAHE + Difference-of-Gaussians preprocessing isolates crack ridges; classical morphology localizes and measures cracks (length, width via distance transform, severity).
  • Union-of-detectors decision driven by cost asymmetry: a missed crack means an expensive return visit; a false positive wastes pennies of sealant.
  • Crack skeleton modeled as a graph; a Chinese Postman route covers every crack segment — the waypoint plan a repair platform follows.
  • Built inside a wider defect-detection framework, with semiconductor (SEM) and aerial inspection as sibling pipelines I'm extending next.

Scan-to-BIM Reconstruction: A Case Study in Honest Evaluation

github ↗
  • Classical geometric pipeline reconstructing multi-storey buildings from raw point-clouds into IFC models: RANSAC plane extraction, normal-based classification, instance splitting, topology refinement.
  • Centerpiece finding: a pipeline reported 99.95% of points classified but was silently mislabeling geometry — The fix was to rely on per-class geometric self-consistency as the honest signal.
  • Writeup covers the audit that caught it, the fix, and the evaluation design. A public-dataset reimplementation is in progress.
